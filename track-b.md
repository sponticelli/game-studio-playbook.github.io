# Track B: Mature Games

For teams working on live games with 1+ years of operational data.
{: .fs-6 .fw-300 }

---

## Overview

Track B focuses on optimizing and improving live game operations. These chapters help you assess game health, improve live ops efficiency, and maintain sustainable operations.

## Chapters

| Block | Chapter | Focus |
|:------|:--------|:------|
| 1 | [Game Health Assessment and Realignment](1B%20-%20Game%20Health%20Assessment%20and%20Realignment/) | Health audit, drift assessment, realignment |
| 2 | [Live Ops Planning and Optimization](2B%20-%20Live%20Ops%20Planning%20and%20Optimization%20%28Mature%20Games%29/) | Roadmap review, capacity analysis, event calendars |
| 3 | [Team Structure for Live Ops](3B%20-%20Team%20Structure%20for%20Live%20Ops%20%28Mature%20Games%29/) | Process audit, team health, rotation |
| 4 | [Operational Excellence](4B%20-%20Operational%20Excellence%20%28Mature%20Games%29/) | Execution efficiency, tech debt, automation |
| 5 | [Scope and Change Management](5B%20-%20Scope%20and%20Change%20Management%20%28Mature%20Games%29/) | Scope discipline, stakeholder reset, priority alignment |
| 6 | [Risk and Problem Solving (Live Games)](6B%20-%20Risk%20and%20Problem%20Solving%20%28Live%20Games%29/) | Operational resilience, burnout prevention, incident response |
| 7 | [Launch, Live Ops and Growth](7B%20-%20Launch%2C%20Live%20Ops%20and%20Growth%20%28Live%20Games%29/) | Sustainability, content rotation, game health reviews |

## When to Use Track B

Use Track B when:
- Your game has been live for 1+ years
- You have substantial player data and analytics
- You're managing ongoing live operations
- You need to assess and improve an existing game's health

If you're starting a new project, use [Track A](track-a/) instead.
